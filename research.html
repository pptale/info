( J-8 ) On the Parameterized Approximability of Contraction to Classes of Chordal Graphs

(in ToCT, APPROX 2020) [Paper, Presentation]

Co-authors: Spoorthy Gunda, Pallavi Jain, Daniel Lokshtanov, Saket Saurabh
Journal: ACM Transactions on Computation Theory (accepted)
Conference: A preliminary version in Approximation, Randomization, and Combinatorial Optimization. Algorithms and Techniques, APPROX/RANDOM 2020
Summary: Clique Contraction is FPT, however, it does not admit a polynomial kernel. Split Contraction is W[1]-Hard, where as Chordal Contraction is W[2]-Hard. Inspired by the intractable results, we study them from the viewpoint of parameterized approximation.

( J-7 ) Paths to Trees and Cacti

(in TCS, CIAC 2017) [Paper, Presentation]

Co-authors: Akanksha Agrawal, Lawqueen Kanesh, Saket Saurabh
Journal: Theoretical Computer Science 860 : 98 − 116, (2021)
Conference: A preliminary version in 10th International Conference on Algorithms and Complexity, CIAC 2017.
Summary: Path-Contraction and Tree-Contraction are FPT but Tree-Contraction does not admit a polynomial kernel while Path-Contraction does. In light of these results, two natural questions are:
1. What additional parameter we can associate with Tree-Contraction such that it admits a polynomial kernel?
2. What additional parameter we can associate with Tree-Contraction such that an FPT algorithm with combination of these parameterizations leads to an algorithm that generalizes the FPT algorithm on trees?
In this paper we focus on the first question. We addressed the second question ( J-3 ).
